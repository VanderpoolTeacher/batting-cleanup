Excited to announce Batting Cleanup — a student-built smart city solution launching in downtown Toledo on Opening Day. The problem: overflowing trash receptacles go unreported for too long. The fix: a simple QR code posted at each location. Anyone who spots a full bin can scan, fill out a short form, and submit — no account, no app, no friction. Every part of this — from the reporting system to the QR poster design to the deployment strategy — was conceived and built by students through Toledo Codes. We're five weeks out from Opening Day activation. We'll be documenting every step.

Post 3 — The Team

Toledo Codes fellows in the field on Adams St., downtown Toledo. This post tells the program story — a University of Toledo fellowship putting students to work on real city problems.

Feature Image
Toledo Codes fellows — Adams St., downtown Toledo
The Fellowship

Toledo Codes is a University of Toledo fellowship that places students in real civic technology projects. Batting Cleanup is the program's most visible deployment — a live smart city solution built from scratch by fellows.

Goal of this post

Introduce the people behind the project. Show that this isn't a corporate product — it's a student-driven initiative backed by a university program with real community impact.

When to post

Week 1–2. Best used as a follow-up to the launch announcement — after people know what Batting Cleanup is, show them who built it.

Location

Adams St. & downtown Toledo — the exact streets where the QR receptacle system is being deployed. Grounding the post in a real place adds authenticity.
